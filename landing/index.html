01 — The point

When your hands are covered in flour you do not want a paragraph. You want a timer that is already running.

Every AI app is the same shape: a box you type into, and text that comes back. That shape is fine for essays. It is a poor tool for the things people actually repeat — dinner, packing, a weekend repair — where the work is structured, the hands are busy, and the answer needs somewhere to live.

Hydropark takes the other road. A skill carries its own interface. Install one and the app grows the panels that task needs, and only those. Remove it and they go away. It is closer to slotting a card into a machine than to opening another chat.

07 — Reasonable questions

Is it as smart as ChatGPT?

No, and it is not trying to be. Hydropark runs a three-billion-parameter model on your processor; ChatGPT runs something hundreds of times larger in a data centre. For “explain quantum tunnelling,” open ChatGPT. For “carbonara for four, start the pasta, show me that in grams” — a structured job with panels and timers, done offline, with the recipe still on screen when you come back — this is the better tool. We chose the job we can win.

Is it really offline?

Yes. After you install it and download the model once, you can disconnect for good. Chatting, every skill you own, timers, lists, unit switching and templates all run locally. The only things that need a connection are buying and downloading new skills. There is no periodic check-in; your license has no expiry date to check.

Do I have to make an account?

Not to use it. The base assistant and the two free skills work the moment you open the app. When you buy, we take your email to send you the download and the unlock code. There is no login, no password to forget.

What actually happens after I pay?

An email arrives with a download link and a one-time unlock code. You install Hydropark, it fetches the AI model on first run — about 2 GB, only ever once — and you paste the code to unlock the Cooking Assistant. Roughly four minutes end to end, most of it the download. We are telling you this because the download is real friction and we would rather you meet it here than be surprised by it after paying.

Which AI is inside, and did you train it?

Qwen2.5-3B-Instruct, an open model released under the Apache-2.0 licence, running through llama.cpp inside the app. We did not train it and we do not fine-tune it on your conversations — we could not, since we never see them. A skill steers the model with a personality and a set of tools; that is all a skill is.

Can a skill do something bad to my computer?

A skill contains no executable code, so there is nothing in it that could run. It is a text description: a personality, which of the app's built-in tools it may call, and which panels to draw. The tools are ours, fixed and audited — start a timer, convert a unit, edit a list. Before you install anything, the app shows you the complete list of what it may touch. Nothing in that catalogue reaches the network or your files.

Is the cooking advice safe to follow?

Treat it as a knowledgeable friend, not an authority. Allergen warnings are produced by a fixed rule from the ingredient list — never written by the AI — but you should still verify allergens and quantities yourself, especially where someone's health depends on it. Nutrition figures are informational and are not medical advice.

What if Hydropark goes out of business?

What you installed keeps working. Licenses are verified against keys already sitting on your machine, so no server has to exist for you to cook. We have committed, in the terms, to shipping a final offline bundle — your skills plus permanent licenses — before any shutdown, and to never releasing our signing key. Owning what you buy is the whole idea; it would be a strange thing to reserve the right to take back.
